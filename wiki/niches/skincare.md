## Real breakouts, torn down
### 877.99x following · 1M+ · belief-challenging
- **Hook technique:** Stacks a curiosity-gap secret + an age qualifier + a specific retailer ('at 50, in Boots') so an older skincare buyer feels it's made for exactly them.
- **Why it spread:** Aspirational age-defying listicle anchored to a real shop: the specificity (Korean, glass skin, 50, Boots) makes it feel actionable, and the high share count signals it traveled as a 'where do I buy this' recommendation.
- **Steal this:** Pin a curiosity-secret to a concrete, recognizable place and a specific age — specificity converts a vague tip into a 'where do I get it' share.

### 692.66x following · 1M+ · belief-challenging
- **Hook technique:** A confident contrarian accusation against an entire industry ('we're being scammed') provokes both agreement and defense, demanding a reaction.
- **Why it spread:** Industry-contrarian storytime: the 'expensive routines are a scam, here's the cheap truth' arc earns trust and 10.4% engagement, with shares from people who feel validated; bare-faced honesty is the proof device.
- **Steal this:** Accuse the whole category of scamming people, then withhold the cheap alternative — the open loop plus your bare-faced proof carries it.

### 650.94x following · 1M+ · information-offering
- **Hook technique:** Reposting a LIVE moment with chat visible borrows the parasocial 'you're in the room' energy and the implied credibility of a real-time crowd asking questions.
- **Why it spread:** LIVE-clip repurposing: clipping a candid stream feels more authentic than a produced video, and the casual TikTok-Shop product mention gets saved as a real-person endorsement.
- **Steal this:** Clip a candid LIVE with the chat still visible — the real-time crowd and unpolished UI make a product mention read as genuine, not an ad.

### 576.78x following · 1M+ · belief-challenging
- **Hook technique:** Reacts to an absurd-looking 10-step nightly routine (chin strap, masks, multiple serums) — the visual excess of the source clip is the scroll-stopper.
- **Why it spread:** Contrarian critique of viral excess: judging an over-engineered routine lets the audience feel sane and validated, generating debate and shares; the visible absurdity of the source carries the reach.
- **Steal this:** React to the most visually excessive example in your niche from a sensible-voice angle — the absurd clip is the hook, your verdict is the share.
