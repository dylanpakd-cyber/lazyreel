## Real breakouts, torn down
### 2010.58x following · 1M+ · size-of-claim
- **Hook technique:** An outsized physiological promise ('arteries withered to a straw forcibly dilated to twice their width in 3 days') with a fear-based symptom callout grabs an older health-anxious viewer.
- **Why it spread:** Classic fear-then-mechanism VSL: it scares, names a 'mechanism' (nitric oxide power-washer), and de-risks with a refund. The 0.75% engagement rate (likes 9k on 1.9M views) is a red flag the reach is paid/boosted, not organic resonance.
- **Steal this:** Only the structure is steal-worthy (fear → named mechanism → guarantee); treat the reach as bought, not proof the creative resonates.

### 1202.89x following · 1M+ · authority
- **Hook technique:** Borrowed authority via storytime — 'I used to work for this doctor who stressed vitamin D for Black women' — anchors the recommendation in a credible third party.
- **Why it spread:** Identity + authority: it speaks directly to Black women's vitamin D deficiency with a doctor's backing, which the community saves (47k) and shares as a trusted PSA; the huge 21% engagement reflects strong in-group resonance.
- **Steal this:** Anchor a health rec in a specific credible third party and a specific identity group — borrowed authority plus belonging drives saves.

### 579.59x following · 10M+ · information-offering
- **Hook technique:** Symptom-mirroring open ('if you take your bra off and everything falls, you need magnesium citrate') makes each viewer self-diagnose, hooking them into their own line of the list.
- **Why it spread:** Diagnostic listicle: personalized symptom callouts maximize saves (91k) as a reference, and the relatable body complaints get shared; modest 2.1% engagement on 14.2M views shows broad reach with light per-view resonance.
- **Steal this:** Build a symptom→solution listicle where each line makes a different viewer point at themselves; that's what drives the save-for-later behavior.
