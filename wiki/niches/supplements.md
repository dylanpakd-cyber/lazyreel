## Real breakouts, torn down
### 2010.58x following · 1M+ · speed-of-claim
- **Hook technique:** Conditional promise with a compressed timeline — opens with 'if you do X, then Y happens in only 3 days,' creating an immediate personal relevance test that stops scrollers who wonder if the claim applies to them
- **Why it spread:** The saves-to-likes ratio (3,461 saves vs 9,095 likes = ~38% save rate) reveals the dominant driver: the video functions as a personal health bookmark. The combination of named ingredients (L-arginine, L-citrulline) plus a refund guarantee converts vague fear into an actionable, credible-sounding protocol people want to retrieve later — saving is self-preservation behavior, not entertainment behavior, and that save volume is what fed the algorithm
- **Steal this:** Name the symptom before naming the product — lead with a specific, embarrassing or frightening physical experience the target audience silently normalizes (numbness, breathlessness, fatigue), let them self-identify as the patient, then introduce the ingredient as the mechanistic answer; the symptom-first structure triggers personal relevance filtering that generic benefit claims skip entirely

### 1202.89x following · 1M+ · authority
- **Hook technique:** Opens with a secondhand authority figure ('a doctor I used to work for') obsessively pushing a specific health message at a specific group — this creates immediate curiosity about why a doctor would care that much, pulling viewers in before any claim is even made.
- **Why it spread:** The video delivers a biologically specific, identity-targeted insight — melanin reduces UV absorption, which structurally predisposes darker-skinned people to vitamin D deficiency — framed as something most of the target audience doesn't know but immediately recognizes as personally relevant. This combination of 'this is about MY body' specificity and genuine information gap triggers saves (to return to) and shares (to warn other Black women), not just passive likes. The caption's direct address ('Black women!! This your reminder') amplifies the share impulse by giving sharers a ready-made reason to send it.
- **Steal this:** Lead with a credentialed third party's disproportionate concern — 'my doctor wouldn't stop bothering me about X' — before making any claim yourself. The borrowed authority lowers skepticism and the behavioral excess (buying bottles for every employee) signals stakes without the creator having to assert them directly.

### 579.59x following · 10M+ · information-offering
- **Hook technique:** Opens with a visceral, mildly embarrassing physical sensation (breast sagging when bra is removed) that is hyper-specific to a female body experience, forcing the target audience to self-identify in the first two seconds before any product is mentioned
- **Why it spread:** The video functions as a diagnostic reference card — it maps specific, named magnesium *types* to specific symptoms, which is information most people have never seen presented this way. The save rate (91,856 saves against 24,500 followers = 3.75x saves-per-follower) confirms viewers are bookmarking it as a resource to return to, not just passively consuming it. Saves then algorithmically signal high-value content, and shares occur because the viewer immediately thinks of a specific person who has that specific symptom — the symptom specificity makes it socially shareable as a recommendation, not just entertainment
- **Steal this:** Lead with one embarrassing or ultra-specific physical symptom your target audience has privately experienced but rarely hears named out loud, before you introduce any product or category — the self-recognition moment creates an immediate parasocial contract that holds attention through the sell

### 566.39x following · 1M+ · comparison
- **Hook technique:** Opens mid-action with a direct product-vs-product comparison frame, naming two specific brands immediately — this signals 'you are about to get a verdict' and activates decision-anxiety in anyone already considering probiotics
- **Why it spread:** The save rate is the telling signal here — 8,827 saves against 9,181 followers means people are bookmarking this as a purchase reference. The video maps a specific ingredient (inulin) to three concrete, embarrassing symptoms (bloating, skin irritation, body acne), which are high-shame, rarely-discussed concerns that people won't comment about publicly but will privately save and share to one trusted person — driving the share number disproportionately through DM rather than public reshare
- **Steal this:** Name the losing product first and let it appear to win on the obvious metric (CFU count), then introduce a single formulation detail (inulin) that reframes the winner — this 'paper vs. reality' flip structure makes viewers feel they learned something non-obvious, which is the primary psychological trigger for saves
