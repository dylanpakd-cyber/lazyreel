## Real breakouts, torn down
### 13565.89x following · 1M+ · direct-callout
- **Hook technique:** A transgressive food act ('pouring matcha on cereal — is this illegal?') triggers disgust-curiosity; you watch to judge whether it's genius or cursed.
- **Why it spread:** Shock-prop + tribe-bait: it fuses the matcha-obsession trend with a polarizing 'is this allowed' food act; the absurd 13.7% engagement and huge share count come from tag-your-friend disgust/delight, not from selling anything.
- **Steal this:** Stage one polarizing visual act and label it with a dare ('is this illegal?') — disgust-curiosity drives shares no product pitch can match.

### 13454.55x following · 10M+ · pov
- **Hook technique:** Pairs a genuinely alarming visual (hospital IV) with a trivial cause ('matcha blocks iron absorption') and the 'you're probably wondering how I ended up here' meme frame, creating instant comedic dissonance.
- **Why it spread:** Meme-format self-deprecation: over-dramatizing a tiny health fact via a recognizable audio makes it endlessly relatable to matcha lovers, driving 222k shares as a 'this is literally me' forward.
- **Steal this:** Dramatize a trivial niche fact with a real high-stakes setting and a known meme audio — the dissonance is the whole joke.

### 1213.41x following · 10M+ · newness
- **Hook technique:** A first-time-trying-it POV plus an instantly funny visceral reaction ('tastes like a vanilla football field... this is grass') hooks both matcha fans and skeptics.
- **Why it spread:** Reaction-bait on a trend's polarizing taste: matcha lovers defend it and haters agree, fueling a 16.8% engagement rate and 581k shares as a comment-war and tag-a-friend video.
- **Steal this:** Film an honest first-time reaction to a polarizing trendy item with quotable similes — defenders and haters both share it to argue.
