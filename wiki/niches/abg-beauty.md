## Real breakouts, torn down
### 1325.58x following · 10M+ · information-offering
- **Hook technique:** She literally paints the 'darkest shade' foundation on as 'black face paint,' turning a shade-range complaint into a visual that you cannot scroll past because you need to know whether she is serious.
- **Why it spread:** It rides an active discourse (foundation shade inclusivity, @golloria) and weaponizes deadpan satire; viewers share to take a side, and the caption 'you all way too dramatic' is itself a quote-able stance that drives the comment war.
- **Steal this:** Don't argue a complaint — physically perform the exaggerated version of it on camera so the rebuttal is a visual the feed can't ignore.

### 1316.18x following · 10M+ · information-offering
- **Hook technique:** Frames itself as a normal 'touch up' tutorial while showing a face that looks anything but finished, so the viewer stays to see whether it's serious, satire, or a trainwreck.
- **Why it spread:** It works as forced-productivity comedy: the value is watching someone perform a beauty ritual with zero polish and full confidence, which reads as funny-relatable and gets stitched/dueted; the absurd length (288s) survives because each segment adds a new bit.
- **Steal this:** Use a familiar tutorial frame ('touch up') but let the visible reality contradict it — the gap between promise and image is the hook.

### 957.42x following · 500k+ · direct-callout
- **Hook technique:** Opens mid-rant with a confrontational direct address ('what the f*** are you doing') that pattern-interrupts a calm beauty feed and dares you to keep watching.
- **Why it spread:** Shock-comedy in a beauty wrapper: the mismatch between sweet GRWM expectations and a profane us-vs-them tirade is inherently shareable to friends ('this is so unhinged'), driving the 10.5% engagement rate on a tiny account.
- **Steal this:** Open on raw confrontational energy, not a greeting — an unhinged direct address out-performs a polite 'hey guys' for scroll-stop.

### 927.88x following · 1M+ · before-after
- **Hook technique:** Pins the video to a high-volume seasonal search ('GRWM for school') so it surfaces to an already-activated audience, then opens on a confident first-day-back premise.
- **Why it spread:** Seasonal trend-surfing: back-to-school GRWM is a recurring high-demand format, and a believable, imperfect teen doing the routine collects mass relatability and saves/shares from the same-age audience.
- **Steal this:** Mirror the exact trending search phrase as on-screen text so the algorithm files you under an active query the audience is already scrolling.
