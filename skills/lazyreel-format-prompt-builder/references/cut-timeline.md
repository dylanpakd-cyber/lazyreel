# Cut-timeline directives

The insight payload for the timeline builder. Every rule maps a validated finding to a timeline decision. Numbers are from the LazyReel corpus (real decoded TikTok and Instagram, past 6 months); the MCP `breakout_laws` and `niche_report` tools serve live versions.

## The cut rhythm is the structure

Per-frame novelty was one of the two strongest things in blind validation. A winner changes what is on screen every few seconds; a static held shot collapses. So the timeline is **one cut per beat**, each a distinct framing, cut every **1.5 to 3 seconds** in the first third, as many cuts as the length needs (a 10-15s video is ~3 to 5; a 60s video is 12 to 18+). The rhythm does not change with length, a longer video is simply more cuts. One continuous clip is the failure mode. This is why the timeline is built in cuts, not as one shot with effects layered on.

## The five laws, as timeline directives

1. **Clip 1 opens on an unresolved visual question**, not a caption that states the premise.
2. **A taste of the payoff lands in clip 1**, the full reveal is withheld to the final cut.
3. **Per-frame novelty:** a new framing every cut.
4. **No format label** (no GRWM / tutorial / review / ad / title card) in the opening. One of the two strongest laws.
5. **Social or emotional charge in clip 1's first frame:** a face, two people, color, or a prop.

## Framing vocabulary (the palette, anti-cinematic)

These are the moves the inventory should use, all achievable as real capture or a cut:
macro insert, reframe (recompose within the action), pov-handheld, two-shot, hands-only, over-the-shoulder, before/after bookend, push-in (subtle, handheld), product-in-action close-up.

Banned (cinematic tells that lose): lens flare, speed ramp, bloom flash, whip pan, crane, dolly, gimbal glide, Dutch angle, color grade, LUT, film grain, slow motion (unless real iPhone slow-mo), heavy bokeh.

## What over-indexes (corpus lift), as timeline cues

- Hooks: speed-of-claim 7.3x, before-after 2.3x. A before-after bookend (cut from the bad state to the result) is one of the most reliable timeline structures.
- Frameworks: problem-agitate-solution 1.8x. Maps cleanly to hook -> agitate -> switch -> payoff.
- Emotion: high-arousal (fear, guilt, nostalgia, disgust) beats neutral. Order the cuts to build the charge.
- Product role: helper 1.6x. Product enters in the middle cuts, named late. Explicit CTA loses (0.67x), so end on the payoff, not a card.
- Combos: pov-handheld + faceless 10.5x. A faceless, handheld, in-the-world timeline can carry the whole piece with no on-camera creator.

## Signature device

Every breakout is built around one cut: the single moment the whole video exists to deliver (the before/after bookend, the murky-to-clear reveal, the 7am/7pm mirror cut). The timeline names it and arranges the other cuts to set it up and pay it off. If there is no signature device, the structure has no spine.

## Sound-off

Most viewers start muted. Clip 1's beat must read as a burned caption with no audio. Write the hook caption-first; the video-editor burns it inside the safe area.
