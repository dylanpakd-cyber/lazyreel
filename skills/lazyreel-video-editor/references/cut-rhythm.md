# Cut rhythm: the measured edit decisions

The edit-side insight payload. Every rule here maps a validated breakout finding to a concrete cut decision. The numbers come from the LazyReel corpus (real decoded TikTok and Instagram). The MCP `breakout_laws` tool serves the live version. Read this before you plan a cut, so the edit follows what wins instead of what feels cinematic.

## Per-frame novelty is the thing (and a single clip kills it)

Law 3 of the first-3-seconds laws, and one of the two strongest in blind validation: a winner changes what is on screen every few seconds. A beauty winner cut to a new macro shot every 7 to 8 seconds across the whole video; the matched dud was a static talking head where nothing changed frame to frame and the view count collapsed.

Edit decision: a 10 to 15 second ad is **3 to 5 clips**, hard-cut, each a distinct framing. Cut every **1.5 to 3 seconds** in the first half, you can stretch to 4 to 5 seconds once the viewer is committed. Never assemble one continuous clip. If you only have one, cut it into multiple framings (reframe, push-in, macro insert) so the screen still changes.

## The hook clip (0 to 3s)

Laws 1, 4, 5. The first clip decides reach. Put your strongest, most unresolved shot first. It opens a loop (a question the viewer needs resolved), front-loads social or emotional charge (a face mid-expression, two people, a color pop, a prop), and carries **no title card and no format label** (no "GRWM", "tutorial", "review", "ad"). Show it, do not label it. Labeling the format in the opening was the single most reliable way to lose in validation.

## Sound-off survival

Most viewers start muted. The first on-screen caption must land the hook on its own, with no audio. Burn captions in (do not rely on platform auto-captions), high-contrast white with an outline, bold, inside the safe area: above the bottom platform UI (roughly the lower 12 to 15 percent) and clear of the right-side action rail. A hook that only works with sound is a hook that does not work.

## 9:16 and the safe area

Vertical 1080x1920. Crop to fill, never letterbox (black bars read as a reposted ad and waste the frame). Keep the subject and any caption inside the safe area: top ~8 percent and bottom ~15 percent can be covered by platform UI, and the right edge holds the action buttons. Center the hook subject.

## Loudness

Normalize to about -14 LUFS integrated, -1.5 dB true peak. A clip that is too quiet gets skipped when the viewer unmutes; one that is too hot is jarring. Keep it consistent across the cut so the seams between clips do not jump.

## Product placement in the cut

Product role "helper" over-indexed 1.6x in winners; product as a background prop under-indexed at 0.73x and an explicit CTA or link-in-bio at 0.67x. So the product enters in clips 2 to 4 as the thing that helps, named late, and the video ends on the payoff, not on a drawn-out call to action. The end is a hard cut on the desirable result, not a logo card.

## Law-to-edit map (quick reference)

- Law 1 (unresolved opening): clip 1 is the most question-raising shot, no explainer caption.
- Law 2 (taste of payoff, withhold): show one striking frame of the payoff early, deliver the full reveal in the final clip.
- Law 3 (per-frame novelty): 3 to 5 clips, cut every 1.5 to 3s.
- Law 4 (no format label): no title card, no "tutorial/review/ad" overlay in the opening.
- Law 5 (social or emotional charge): open on a face, people, color, or a prop, not a neutral empty frame.

## What over-indexes, as edit cues

- Hooks: speed-of-claim 7.3x, before-after 2.3x. A before-after edit (cut from the bad state straight to the result) is one of the most reliable cut structures.
- Emotion: high-arousal (fear, guilt, nostalgia, disgust) beats neutral. Pick the clip order that builds the charge.
- Combos: pov-handheld and faceless over-indexed 10.5x. You do not need a creator on camera; a faceless, handheld, in-the-world cut can carry the whole piece.
